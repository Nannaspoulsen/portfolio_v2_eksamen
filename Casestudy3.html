HÖJSKOLENDK

HÖJSKOLENDK is a Danish folk high school offering travel-based learning programs. This project took place before I started studying Multimedia Design. I joined as a helper on the website design and it was my first hands-on experience with digital design. At the time, I had no formal design training, but I was deeply curious about how visuals and words could shape emotion and clarity.

1. Early Exploration

My role began with helping to organize the website content and visual direction. I worked closely with the team behind HÖJSKOLENDK to figure out how to communicate their main ideas: community, travel and curiosity about the world. Most of my decisions were based on intuition: what felt clear, warm, and human.

2. Finding Structure

The site needed to balance practical information (trips, prices, travel details) with emotional storytelling. I created simple drafts for the navigation, mainly by observing what users asked about most often. This became my first understanding of what I now know as information architecture.

3. Visual Design

I helped choose photos, colors, and typography that reflected openness and authenticity. We wanted the site to feel like the beginning of a journey: full of light, connection, and adventure. Even without knowing about design principles or UX, I naturally focused on harmony and atmosphere.

4. Learning through Practice

The developer on the team built the site and I contributed design suggestions, text edits, and layout adjustments. Seeing my ideas turn into a functioning website sparked my interest in digital communication and eventually led me to study Multimedia Design.

Reflection and Learning

Looking back, this project marks the start of everything I do today. I didn’t yet know terms like “user experience” or “visual hierarchy,” but I began to sense their importance. It taught me that design is not just about looks - it’s about making people feel connected and informed.
