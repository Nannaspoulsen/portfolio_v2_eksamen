The process

1. Research & User Insights

We begin by understanding the context. Who are your users? What do they need? I conduct market research, segment the audience, and gather insights through observations and interviews. Examples: desk research, audience segmentation, user needs.

2. Empathy & Inspiration

I translate research into vision and emotional direction. Tools like empathy-maps and mood boards help define the tone, feel and overall attitude of the design. Examples: empathy mapping, moodboard, design guidelines.

3. Structure & Content

I organize content so it's logical and user-friendly. I create a content system and clear content sorting that guides users to an intuitive information architecture. Examples: content strategy, navigation flow, card sorting.

4. Content Modeling & Classification

I structure the types of content we'll be working with - for example, recipes or blog posts - and define their features and fields. That makes it easier for users to browse and find exactly what they're looking for. Examples: metadata, faceted filters, taxonomies.

5. Sketching & Prototyping

With structure in place, I bring ideas to life visually. This includes sketches, wireframes and clickable mockups — all shaped around the user’s needs. The design evolves through several iterations. Examples: low-fi sketches, UI patterns, motion design, high-fi mockups.

6. User Testing

I test the design with real users using methods like 5-second tests and think-aloud testing. Their feedback helps refine functionality, navigation and overall user experience. Examples: usability testing, Think Aloud, feedback-based revisions.

7. Development & Integration

Once the design is validated, I develop the solution using HTML, CSS and JavaScript. I use WordPress as headless CMS and integrate content via REST API for flexibility and control. Examples: custom fields in WordPress, REST API setup, dynamic rendering with JavaScript.

8. Launch & Scale

Your solution goes live. From here, we can scale and grow as needed. I offer ongoing support and training so you feel confident updating your content and design forward. Examples: launch support, self-editing guides, options for future development.
