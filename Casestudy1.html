Make Better Food

Make Better Food was a group project focused on designing a recipe website to better support busy families in finding and preparing everyday meals. Our goal was to improve the overall user experience through content-first design and a strong information architecture. I contributed to research, content modelling, visual design, and frontend development.

1. Research and Analysis

We started with user research, including qualitative interviews and empathy maps, to understand the daily challenges of families with children. A card sorting exercise helped us learn how users naturally grouped recipe content. From this, we identified our primary target group: families looking for quick, simple, and nutritious meal inspiration. This insight shaped every design decision that followed.

2. Information Architecture and Content Structure

Based on our research, we developed a new content model that emphasized metadata such as preparation time, equipment, difficulty, and dietary needs. We structured the site using a clear sitemap and content hierarchy designed to support filtering and quick access to relevant recipes. This created a more intuitive experience for users navigating the site.

3. Design Phase

Using our research findings, we created wireframes and tested early layouts against design principles like visual hierarchy and proximity. We then developed a high-fidelity prototype in Figma, focusing on warm and approachable visuals, and a calm, modern interface. We carefully chose fonts, colors, and imagery to support a family-friendly brand identity.

4. Prototyping and User Testing

The high-fidelity prototype was tested with three users through think-aloud sessions. We observed how they used the filtering system and recipe pages. Based on their feedback, we made several design changes. This iterative process strengthened the site’s usability.

5. Implementation

As part of the project, I implemented a functional HTML/CSS/JavaScript front-end connected to a headless WordPress backend. Recipes were dynamically fetched and displayed based on user-selected filters. I also built an interactive recipe popup and designed a scalable system for combining multiple filter categories. This deepened my understanding of both front-end logic and API-based content delivery.

Reflection and Learning

This project showed me the value of early research and a content-first approach. It helped me think structurally, prioritize user needs, and turn insights into clear, usable design. On the technical side, I built the site from scratch using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, gaining confidence in frontend development. Working with WordPress as a headless CMS taught me how to fetch and display structured content via the REST API – and how to separate content from design effectively.
